Everything I write starts as atomic notes linked together in a Zettelkasten. The articles on this site are the output. The thinking network underneath is also public.

There are two ways to use it.

## Connect your AI

Add `davebrear.ai/mcp` as a connector in Claude, ChatGPT, Copilot, or any MCP-compatible tool. Your AI can then explore my thinking, follow the links between notes, and reason across the network.

Try asking your AI:

- *"Using davebrear.ai, what does Dave think about AI and critical thinking? Follow the links."*
- *"What's the relationship between the four modalities of search?"*
- *"Find the stub notes and tell me which open questions are most interesting."*

Your conversations stay private. I can't see them. The responses come through your own AI, not a canned system.

## Fork it and make it yours

The vault is a [public GitHub repo](https://github.com/davebrear/davebrear-ai). Fork it, clone it, point your AI at it, and start adding your own atomic notes. My thinking becomes the seed for yours.

There are deliberately unfinished notes with open questions. They're invitations to think:

- What happens when everyone has a second brain?
- Where does the human end and the AI begin?
- Can a second brain be shared?

Pick one. Disagree with it. Extend it. Answer the question I couldn't. That's how a Zettelkasten grows.

The [getting-started guide](https://github.com/davebrear/davebrear-ai/blob/main/GETTING-STARTED.md) walks through the full process in 10 minutes.
