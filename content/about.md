I'm a technology strategist at a large software vendor, helping customers get the most out of their investment in our technologies. I'm also unreasonably excited about what AI is doing to knowledge work right now. The kind of excited where you connect an AI to your notes at 9am and look up at 1pm wondering where the morning went.

This site is a record of that excitement turning into practice: how my understanding has developed, what I've built along the way, and how you can apply these ideas in your own workflows.

## What I write about

Most of what you'll find here sits at the intersection of three things I care about:

**Knowledge management.** I use a Zettelkasten-inspired note system (Obsidian, plain markdown, wiki-links) as a thinking environment. Not a filing system. The notes accumulate, connect, and eventually produce writing as a byproduct.

**AI as a thinking partner.** Not AI as a content factory. I'm interested in how AI agents can work with a structured knowledge base to expand thinking rather than replace it. The process matters more than the output.

**The provenance problem.** In a world where anyone can generate polished text in seconds, how do you prove that real thinking happened? I'm developing ideas around what I call a *chain of cognition*: using the evolution trail of your notes as proof of thought.
